Research Interest

Mathematical biology and related mathematical subjects

Chemical reaction network theory (CRN)

Under the well-mixed assumption, the amount of chemical species can mathematically described by deterministic model (ODEs) and stochastic model (continuous-time Markov chain).

By simply looking at the chemical reaction network, we can find the properties of that reaction network. One of my fundamental question lies : How does biochemical reaction network maintains homeostasis? How does the structure of reaction network affects the homeostasis? Can we construct a reaction network to obtain homeostasis?

Continous-time Markov chain

Homeostasis of deterministic CRN is well-known. However, the homeostasis of stochastic CRN is not well-known yet. For example, even though some reaction network has stable steady-state in deterministic condition, we don't know whether it is also stable in stochastic condition.

Homeostasis of stochastic CRN corresponds to ergodicity, exponential ergodicity and its properties in the stationary distribution. I'm interested in finding those properties in chemical reaction network.

Evolutionary dynamics and PDE-based mathematical biology

One of the major problem of CRN is the well-mixed assumption. In real life, almost every environment are not well-mixed. To consider the spatial dynamics, it is crucial to describe models in PDE.

In this aspect, I'm interested in the relationship between evolutionary game theory and PDE, especially how the strategy of evolutionary game theory affect its dynamics in the PDE limit. At the end, I believe that CRN-based dynamics, evolutionary game-based dynamics, and PDE-based dynamics could coincide in some sense.
