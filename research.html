Research Interest

Mathematical biology and related mathematical subjects

Chemical reaction network theory (CRN)

Under the well-mixed assumption, the amount of chemical species can mathematically described by deterministic model (ODEs) and stochastic model (continuous-time Markov chain).

By simply looking at the structure of a reaction network, we can know its dynamical properties by the theorems in CRN. One of my fundamental question lies : How does biochemical reaction network maintains homeostasis? How does the structure of reaction network affects the homeostasis? Can we construct some reaction network to obtain homeostasis?

Continous-time Markov chain

Homeostasis of deterministic CRN is well-known. However, the homeostasis of stochastic CRN is not well-known yet. For example, even though some reaction network has stable steady-state in deterministic condition, we don't know whether it also has such stationary distribution in stochastic condition.

Homeostasis of stochastic CRN corresponds to the ergodicity of continuous-time Markov chain. Exponential/Nonexponential ergodicity or the properties in the stationary distribution are the related topics. I'm interested in finding those properties in the continuous-time Markov chain.

Evolutionary dynamics and PDE-based mathematical biology

One problem of CRN is the well-mixed assumption. In real life, many environments are not well-mixed. To consider the spatiality, it is crucial to describe dynamics using PDE.

In this aspect, I'm interested in the relationship between evolutionary game theory and PDE, especially how the strategy of evolutionary game theory affect its dynamics in the PDE limit. At the end, I believe that CRN-based dynamics, EGT-based dynamics, and PDE-based dynamics could coincide in some sense.
